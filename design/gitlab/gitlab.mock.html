GitLab integration — connect / settings · project selection · MR & pipeline status

Story 7.23 · MOTIR-1472 (design gate). The mirror of 7.10 (GitHub, MOTIR-889) for GitLab — the second provider behind the shared GitProvider seam, so both render through ONE connect-settings surface with a provider picker (the shipped Segmented [GitHub | GitLab]). Designed against shipped reality — the HONEST differences: GitLab is a single OAuth authorization (not GitHub's two grants); project selection is in-app (the OAuth token can enumerate + webhook your projects — the honest inverse of GitHub's external install screen); terminology is Merge Request (!123) and Pipeline. Placement is the same Settings → Workspace → Git shell. Composed only from shipped primitives — Card, Pill, Button, EmptyState, Switch, Segmented, the settings-area shell — colour via --el-*, shape via element-semantic tokens; the GitLab mark is monochrome currentColor (no invented brand hex).

Step 1 · Authorize

Connect your GitLab account

Authorize Motir on GitLab in one step. This confirms who you are and grants API access to the projects you’re a member of — Motir reads merge requests and pipelines and adds webhooks only on the projects you connect next. One grant covers identity and access — there’s no separate app to install.

read_user read_api api
Step 2 · Projects

Choose projects in Motir

After you authorize, pick which of your GitLab projects to sync — right here in Motir, not on a separate screen. Motir only touches the projects you connect, and you can disconnect any of them any time.

You’ll be sent to GitLab to authorize, then choose projects here.
