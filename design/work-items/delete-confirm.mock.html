Entry points are admin/permitted only; the confirm warns it can't be undone, names the cascade count, and offers Archive as the recoverable alternative. Reuses the 5.3.6 delete-with-count dialog grammar.

Two destructive actions, one separator apart

Archive (neutral row) hides the item but keeps it — reversible. Delete… (danger row, trailing ellipsis = opens a confirm) is permanent. The danger styling + ellipsis + separator make the two impossible to confuse. Both rows appear ONLY for users who hold the matching permission (panel 1).

The ⋯ menu is the shipped Popover actions menu (RowActionsMenu vocabulary). Delete is the only red row; its trailing … signals a confirm step follows (never deletes on click). Same menu, same order, on every surface below.

2 · Confirm dialog — parent with descendants (the cascade)

Delete this work item?

This permanently deletes MOTIR-142 “Saved filters — apply, manage & subscribe” and everything beneath it. This can't be undone.

  • 7 descendants will also be deleted — 5 subtasks, 1 task, 1 bug.
  • All comments, attachments, and activity history on these 8 items go with them.
  • Links from other items (blocks, relates to, duplicates) are removed.
Just want it out of the way? Archive hides this item but keeps it and its history — and leaves its descendants live. You can restore it later.
The 5.3.6 delete-with-count grammar

A real role="alertdialog". The cascade count is the headline; consequences are named in text, never colour-only. The destructive button states the magnitude — “Delete 8 items” = the item + its 7 descendants — so the count is on the button you press, not just in the prose. Archive sits inside the same dialog as the one-click safe exit.

The cascade numbers come from a subtree count the dialog reads before opening (2.8.x backend); the per-kind breakdown mirrors Jira's "this issue has N sub-tasks" warning. The button count = 1 + descendants. Default focus lands on Cancel, and Esc / the ✕ both cancel — the safe default for a destructive dialog.

3 · Confirm dialog — leaf item (no descendants)

Delete this work item?

This permanently deletes MOTIR-211 “Burndown chart data endpoint”. It has no child items. This can't be undone.

  • Its comments, attachments, and activity history are deleted too.
Prefer to keep it recoverable? Archive hides it from active views but keeps the item and its history. You can restore it later.
No cascade → no count list

When the item is a leaf, the cascade row drops out, the copy says “It has no child items”, and the button reads “Delete work item” (no magnitude to name). The irreversibility warning, the comments/history note, and the Archive alternative stay — those are true for every delete.

6 · 2.9.7 — deleting an item from the ARCHIVED view, when it still owns LIVE descendants

Delete this work item?

This permanently deletes PROD-49 “OAuth callback drops the `state` param on Safari ≤16” and everything beneath it. This can't be undone.

Some of what's beneath this isn't archived. Deleting also permanently removes 5 active work items that aren't archived — 4 subtasks, 1 task. They're still live on your boards and lists. (Archiving an item never archives its children.)
  • All comments, attachments, and activity history on these 6 items go with them.
  • Links from other items (blocks, relates to, duplicates) are removed.
The one new case 2.9.7 designs

Archive is single-node — archiving this item never archived its children, so they're still live on the boards. Deleting the archived parent cascades to them anyway. The peach warning names the live count (here all 5 descendants are live), distinct from the amber descendant rows and the mint archive callout — so the surprise is impossible to miss. There is no Archive escape hatch here: the item is already archived.

The live count is the non-archived subset of the subtree (it can be fewer than the total descendants when some children were archived individually). Data note for 2.9.5: getDeletePreview (workItemsService.ts:1614) returns total / descendant / per-kind today but does NOT split archived vs live — 2.9.5 extends it with a live-(non-archived)-descendant count + breakdown to drive this copy. The button count is the whole subtree (1 + descendants = 6), unchanged.

7 · 2.9.7 — the contrast: an archived parent whose descendants are ALL archived too (no live items at risk → no peach warning)

Delete this work item?

This permanently deletes PROD-28 “SAML SSO (dropped from Q3 scope)” and everything beneath it. This can't be undone.

  • 3 descendants will also be deleted — 2 subtasks, 1 task. All of them are already archived — nothing here is live on your boards.
  • All comments, attachments, and activity history on these 4 items go with them.
  • Links from other items (blocks, relates to, duplicates) are removed.
All-archived → no surprise

When the live-descendant count is 0 (every descendant is archived too), the peach warning is suppressed — there are no active items to lose, so the dialog is the ordinary cascade confirm, with a calm note that everything here is already archived. This is the boundary that makes panel 6's warning meaningful. An archived leaf (no descendants) reuses the leaf dialog (panel 3) unchanged.
