Epic-level privacy on public projects

Story 6.14 · subtask 6.14.1 — on a public project, an admin marks an EPIC private: its row stays visible as a placeholder, but its children + aggregate tells are hidden from non-members (server-side). Members see everything.

Panel 1 — Public viewer · the private-epic ROW · title + “Not public” badge, NO child count / progress / points — contrasted with a normal epic row that shows all three

The child count, progress meter, and point total are stripped server-side in the 6.12.4 public projection — they are absent from the response payload, not merely hidden in the DOM. The epic row itself is never a 404; it stays as a deliberate “there is an epic here, its details are private” placeholder.

The toggle is the shipped Switch primitive (role="switch"). It is shown in the epic’s settings / actions area and is project-admin-gated — a non-admin member sees it read-only (panel 6c), never hidden, so the state is legible to everyone with access.

The quiet “Members only” chip (neutral tone, distinct from the public “Not public” lavender badge) signals the epic IS private to the public while making clear the member can see it — mirroring Jira showing a security-level marker to those cleared for it.
