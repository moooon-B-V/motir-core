Every AI-planning surface (generation review 7.4, re-planning 7.11, contextual planning 7.12, the persistent roadmap 7.19, and onboarding 7.3) composes the same full-screen canvas (left) + chat (right) shell — they are MODES (states) of this one surface, not separate UIs. This asset designs the workspace shell + its behaviours + the universal entrance — two always-present hero affordances (a header “Plan with AI” pill + a floating M AI callout) that restyle with the active design style (sheet 5). The canvas pane is the standalone WorkItemCanvas (MOTIR-1194; design @ design/roadmap/) — composed here, never redrawn.

Style-aware — the “Plan with AI” control looks special in every design style

restyles with data-style

Motir dogfoods its own swappable style axis (data-style). The hero control is a style-aware surface: it doesn’t just inherit a token — each style gives it a distinct, special treatment (gradient + glow, frosted glass, hard-shadow brutalist, neon, flat-Swiss, soft-playful), with colour palette-derived so a data-palette swap re-tints it and a data-style swap re-shapes it. The same logic themes the floating M orb.

How it stays token-disciplined. Like the sanctioned surface-material styles (glassmorphism & co.), the hero control gets a per-style material treatment — [data-style='id'] [data-surface='ai-cta'] {…} — whose colour is palette-derived (color-mix() / var(--el-accent | --el-highlight), no raw hex), and whose radius / padding / shadow flow through the element-semantic shape tokens. So the two axes stay disjoint: a palette swap re-tints every style’s treatment; a style swap re-shapes it. AA holds in each — label text sits over the accent-dominant region (and Cybercore renders its native dark register).

Opening & exiting — a full-screen overlay ON TOP of the app, with a clear close

layer, not a route change

The workspace covers the screen (the canvas + chat need the room) but it is a full-screen overlay LAYERED ON TOP of the PM app — not a navigation away. The app stays mounted, dimmed, behind it; closing returns you to the exact screen you launched from (same scroll, same filters), with no reload. So it is “full-screen” for working and “on top” for context.

Why an overlay, not a route. The summoned planner is a session on top of your work, not a place you navigate to — so it mounts as a full-screen layer above the current route (the app inert behind), and closing restores that route instantly with no reload or lost state. The close lives in the workspace’s own top-left chrome (✕ + Esc, labelled with the origin); a dismissal with unconfirmed proposals hits the confirm-to-persist guard (Discard / Keep planning / Confirm & add). Onboarding is the one case that is a genuine full-page route (a dedicated first-run journey), not this dismissable overlay.
